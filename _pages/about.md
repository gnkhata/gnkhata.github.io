Hi, I’m Gibson Nkhata — a Ph.D. candidate in Computer Science at the University of Arkansas, researcher, and AI engineer passionate about building intelligent, trustworthy, and scalable AI systems, advised by [Prof. Susan Gauch](https://engineering.uark.edu/electrical-engineering-computer-science/electrical-engineering-faculty/uid/sgauch/name/Susan+E+Gauch/). My research lies at the intersection of Natural Language Processing (NLP), Large Language Models (LLMs), Retrieval-Augmented Generation (RAG), information retrieval, and trustworthy AI. I work on developing robust machine learning systems for conversational understanding, personalized retrieval, misinformation detection, and language-based reasoning.

My recent research includes ClusterRAG, a cluster-based collaborative filtering framework for personalized retrieval-augmented generation accepted to ACL 2026, as well as work on rumor verification, stance-aware conversational modeling, and neural retrieval systems using transformer architectures and deep learning. I am particularly interested in language model evaluation, AI safety, retrieval systems, and human-centered AI for high-impact real-world applications.

I have experience designing end-to-end AI pipelines, including large-scale data processing, distributed model training, neural retrieval/ranking, and deployment-oriented machine learning workflows using technologies such as PyTorch, Hugging Face Transformers, TensorFlow, and GPU computing infrastructure. Beyond research, I enjoy mentoring students, collaborating across disciplines, and contributing to the AI research community through peer review and open scientific collaboration.

This website highlights my research, publications, projects, presentations, and ongoing work in AI, NLP, and machine learning.
